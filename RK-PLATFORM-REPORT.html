4. Key Stakeholder Quotes & Feedback-Driven Changes

Approval Workflow Engine — Score: 9.3/10

"Check runs get buried. Quotes get buried. If it's in guidelines, bang bang bang — I shouldn't even need to look at it. This is critical." — Joe Maclean, CEO
"Check runs are my biggest pain point. If Joe can approve from his dashboard instead of searching through email, we save 2-3 days per cycle." — David Chen, Controller

Changes Made Based on Feedback:

  • Mobile one-tap approval via push notification
  • Escalation timers — auto-escalate to VP if CEO doesn't respond within configurable window
  • Batch approval for multiple in-guideline items
  • Quote status tracker visible to submitting BD rep
  • Immutable audit log with export capability for compliance
  • Dashboard widget implemented — Approval queue now visible on CEO Dashboard

Contract & Lease Intelligence — Score: 9.3/10 (↑ Upgraded to Tier 1)

"This is exactly what I was talking about on the call. Every contract, every building, every escalation date. The Netflix one March 1 — we can't miss those." — James Bryant, VP Operations
"You have no idea how much time I spend on this. If the system tells me 'Netflix escalation is in 15 days,' that changes my entire workflow." — Susanna Martinez, Ops Admin

Changes Made Based on Feedback:

  • Upgraded from Tier 2 to Tier 1 — unanimously scored 10/10 urgency by Operations personas
  • AI-powered contract PDF parser for bulk onboarding
  • Simple, non-technical form interface designed for Susanna's daily use
  • Color-coded calendar view (green/yellow/red for deadline proximity)
  • Revenue impact projection for upcoming escalations
  • Dashboard widget implemented — Escalation tracker now visible on CEO Dashboard

Personalized BD Dashboards — Score: 8.4/10

"If I can see my clients, create quotes, and update pipeline in one place, I'll actually use it. Right now I'm in 4 different tools." — Brian Saucier, BD Rep

Changes Made Based on Feedback:

  • Mobile-responsive design as hard requirement (CEO and reps need mobile access)
  • Rep performance leaderboard with opt-in visibility to address surveillance concern
  • Daily AI briefing module per rep with top 3 priorities
  • Email tracking limited to metadata only (volume, recency) — not content — per rep feedback

Email/Activity Intelligence — Score: 5.9/10 (Redesigned)

"Honestly, this makes me uncomfortable. If it's about tracking my email count, it doesn't capture the two-hour phone call I had with a prospect." — Brian Saucier, BD Rep

Critical Redesign Based on Feedback:

  • Reframed from "Email Monitoring" to "Activity Intelligence" — reps perceived it as surveillance
  • Multi-channel tracking: email + phone + meetings + self-reported activities
  • Rep sees their own dashboard first — management sees aggregates and anomalies only
  • "Customer last contacted" metric as primary use case, not rep monitoring
  • Lowered priority — build after BD dashboards are adopted and trust is established

Communication Hub — Score: 5.3/10 (Deprioritized)

"Another messaging tool? I'm already in email, text, and Teams. Unless it replaces ALL of them, it's just one more thing to check." — Brian Saucier, BD Rep

Pivot Based on Feedback:

  • Full communication hub abandoned — adoption risk too high (competes with email/Teams)
  • Pivoted to "Notification Center" only — aggregate alerts from all platform modules
  • Daily digest email as bridge for non-adopters
  • In-platform messaging removed from scope entirely

9. Cross-Cutting Themes from Audience Feedback

MOBILE-FIRST (5 mentions)

CEO, BD reps, and warehouse managers all need mobile access. One-tap approvals, push notifications, and responsive design are non-negotiable.

PRIVACY & TRUST (3 mentions)

Email monitoring reframed as Activity Intelligence. Metadata only. Rep-facing dashboards shown first, management sees aggregates. Camera AI needs transparent employee communication.

PHASED ROLLOUT (4 mentions)

SaaS elimination needs 90-day parallel runs. Camera AI pilots one facility first. TMS starts read-only. Email intelligence builds after BD dashboard trust.

SIMPLICITY FOR NON-TECHNICAL USERS (3 mentions)

Susanna needs simple forms, not complex interfaces. Warehouse managers need practical views, not executive dashboards. Customer portal must be intuitive.

REVENUE PROTECTION (2 mentions)

Contract escalation enforcement and approval workflow acceleration both directly protect and accelerate revenue capture. These moved to Phase 1 as a result.
